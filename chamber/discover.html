Discover Kumasi

Spatial and Population Analysis

Covering an area of 8.8 kmÂ², Kumasi accommodates a diverse population of 16,879 individuals. The median age in Kumasi is 36.5 years, with males averaging 39.7 years and females 46.3 years. Positioned strategically, Kumasi is in the Asante region of Ghana.

Population Trends and Density

Kumasi has sustained a relatively stable population over the years, experiencing a slight increase from 1957 to 2024. Despite minor fluctuations, the population density has consistently ranged between 1960 and 2002 individuals per square kilometer, indicating a compact yet lively community..

Economic Benefits

Kumasi plays a crucial role in providing food and educational materials to those in need, serving as a pivotal hub for industry, commerce, education, and services, with well-established educational links. Kumasi enhances its connectivity and accessibility, facilitating the distribution of essential resources..

Attractions and Cultural Sites

Uncover Kumasi's captivating attractions by delving into the enchanting old town, where cobblestone streets guide you to architectural marvels like Kumasi chief places and Otumfoc status.

Calendar

The calendar of Kumasi is filled with vibrant cultural festivals like Akwasidae and Adae, honoring the Ashanti king and ancestral spirits. Religious celebrations such as Eid al-Fitr, Eid al-Adha, Christmas, and Easter bring communities together. Additionally, there are community gatherings, sports tournaments, and educational seminars throughout the year, fostering unity and celebration in this bustling Ghanaian city!
